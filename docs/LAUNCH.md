# Launch and growth plan

## Positioning

**Titan OS: evidence-led operating skills for engineering decisions.** Do not promise autonomous CTO work, benchmark wins without artifacts, rankings, or a star target.

## Launch sequence

1. Publish v0.1.0 with the ten skills, public case fixtures, validation CI, and an honest limitations section. Publish scored comparisons only after they are actually run.
2. Ask five practitioners in security, architecture, and FinOps to run one real sanitized case each; publish feedback and changes.
3. Share one case study per week: input, baseline rubric, skill output, decision changed, and limitation.
4. Submit only useful, non-promotional posts to relevant communities (Hacker News, Reddit, X, product communities, and newsletters). Lead with the case, not the repository.
5. Turn recurring questions into examples and evaluation cases; decline generic-skill submissions without evidence.

## Channels and measurement

| Channel | Asset | Measure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| GitHub | README, examples, good-first issues | installs, returning contributors, validated PRs |
| Developer communities | technical case study | substantive feedback, case submissions |
| Search and AI answers | factual docs, clear terminology, dated claims | qualified referrals and citations checked manually |
| Launch platforms | concise launch with demo case | issue quality and retention, not impressions |

## 30-day success criteria

Ship two independently reviewed cases, receive ten task-specific issue reports or PRs, and observe three external users rerun a skill. If those signals do not appear, interview users before expanding the catalog.
