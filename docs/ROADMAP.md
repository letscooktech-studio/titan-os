# 12-month roadmap

## Q1 — prove usefulness

Publish the first ten skills, three sanitized evaluation cases for the two highest-risk skills, a compatibility matrix, and a contributor onboarding path. Target: 20 useful external issue reports or PRs, not a vanity star count.

## Q2 — earn trust

Add adapters for high-demand agent surfaces, publish benchmark runs with versioned artifacts, and establish maintainers for security, architecture, and cost domains.

## Q3 — compound the corpus

Release an evaluation runner, accept community cases under a privacy policy, and build case studies showing a changed engineering decision rather than a pretty response.

## Q4 — scale responsibly

Form a maintainer council, introduce semantic versioning and deprecation policy, and test launches in developer communities where feedback is specific and public.

## Growth principles

Show real inputs and scored outcomes. Launch a small number of strong examples repeatedly. Do not buy attention, manufacture benchmarks, or position the project as a replacement for engineering judgment.

## Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Model/vendor churn | Portable Markdown source and thin adapters. |
| Generic-content sprawl | Evaluation gate and owner requirement. |
| Unsafe advice | Scope statements, high-stakes review, and safety tests. |
| Maintainer burnout | Small initial surface area and explicit deprecation. |
| Growth without trust | Evidence-led case studies and transparent limitations. |
