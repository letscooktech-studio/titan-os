# Competitor Analyzer

Apply the shared [Titan skill standard](../../docs/SKILL-STANDARD.md) for evidence, verification, risk, benchmarking, and improvement.

Build a decision-ready view of customer alternatives, not a feature checklist.

## Workflow

1. Define the buyer, job, geography, segment, and decision context; include “do nothing” and internal build as competitors.
2. Assemble dated, attributable evidence from product docs, pricing, changelogs, customer reviews, demos, and interviews.
3. Compare alternatives across job completion, switching cost, distribution, integration depth, pricing metric, trust, and defensibility.
4. Identify gaps that buyers demonstrably value; distinguish a temporary feature gap from a durable wedge.
5. Recommend one positioning claim, one proof mechanism, and one experiment per opportunity.

## Output contract

Return a market definition, evidence ledger, competitor matrix, battlecards, opportunity ranking (demand, differentiation, reachability, execution cost, durability), and a recommendation. Attach a source and date to each external claim.

## Quality gates

- Do not infer revenue, users, or roadmap from weak signals.
- Flag stale pricing and feature evidence.
- Include disconfirming evidence for the favored strategy.
- Say “unknown” rather than manufacturing precision.
