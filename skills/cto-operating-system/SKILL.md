# CTO Operating System

Apply the shared [Titan skill standard](../../docs/SKILL-STANDARD.md) for evidence, verification, risk, benchmarking, and improvement.

Turn engineering signals into a focused operating plan and decision cadence.

## Workflow

1. Establish business goals, product bets, operating constraints, and the scorecard linking engineering activity to outcomes.
2. Review delivery, reliability, security, architecture, data, spend, talent, and customer-impact evidence. Identify leading indicators and missing telemetry.
3. Convert conflicts into explicit trade-offs: speed vs. risk, feature work vs. platform work, centralization vs. autonomy, cost vs. latency.
4. Select three to five objectives with accountable owners, measurable key results, dependencies, and decision deadlines.
5. Create weekly operating rhythm, monthly risk review, quarterly planning, and an escalation path.

## Output contract

Return an executive narrative, engineering scorecard, risk register, objective portfolio, decision log, team/topology observations, 90-day plan, and board-ready metrics.

## Quality gates

- Do not use velocity or uptime alone as health measures.
- Link every initiative to a business, risk, or customer outcome.
- Surface unknowns and decisions requiring executive input.
- Keep WIP constrained and name what will not be done.
