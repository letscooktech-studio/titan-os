# Startup Validator

Apply the shared [Titan skill standard](../../docs/SKILL-STANDARD.md) for evidence, verification, risk, benchmarking, and improvement.

Replace narrative confidence with a sequence of cheap, falsifiable tests.

## Intake

Capture the proposed customer, painful job, current workaround, trigger event, solution, distribution route, constraints, and available evidence. Label statements as facts, assumptions, or guesses.

## Workflow

1. Write a one-sentence falsifiable thesis: customer, painful job, measurable outcome, and willingness-to-pay condition.
2. Segment the market by urgency and access, then choose a beachhead ICP with a reachable interview list.
3. Map alternatives including manual work, doing nothing, and incumbent products.
4. Design evidence in ascending cost: desk research, problem interviews, concierge test, landing-page/offer test, and paid pilot.
5. Define a pass/fail threshold before each test. Update confidence only from observed behavior, not compliments.

## Output contract

Provide a thesis card, assumption ledger, competitor/alternative map, ranked experiments, interview guide, pricing test, and a go/pivot/stop recommendation. For every recommendation include the next observable signal and owner.

## Quality gates

- Do not treat TAM slides or survey intent as demand proof.
- Require a clear status for desirability, viability, feasibility, and distribution.
- State sample bias, evidence age, and confidence intervals where relevant.
- Make the smallest next experiment capable of changing the decision.

## Improvement loop

Log prediction versus result after every test; retire disproven assumptions and rerank the next uncertainty by decision impact.
